Ecological Significance

The Okapi Wildlife Reserve (OWR) occupies about one-fifth of the Ituri Forest. It protects a rich diversity of wildlife and provides a crucial carbon sink for the planet.

Endemic Species

Home to one of the largest remaining populations of okapi, forest elephants, and chimpanzees.

Indigenous Culture

Supporting the traditional nomadic Mbuti and Efe hunters who have lived here for millennia.

Methodology

Our analysis utilizes Global Forest Watch (GFW) satellite monitoring data. The statistics presented here are specific to the Okapi Wildlife Reserve (RFO) administrative boundaries and its surrounding 50km buffer zone (sourced from OWR GFW.csv).

Satellite Imagery

Using Landsat and Sentinel-2 data to track changes in tree cover canopy with 30m resolution.

Annual Processing

Data is processed annually to identify deforestation hotspots and evaluate conservation impact.

Landsat Spectral Analysis

Analyzing forest cover through multispectral Landsat satellite bands to identify vegetation patterns and forest health indicators.

NDVI Index

Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) calculated from Red and NIR bands indicates vegetation greenness and health. Higher values = healthier vegetation.

Vegetation Classification

SWIR bands combined with visible wavelengths help distinguish forest types, degradation levels, and land cover changes with high precision.
