⚠️ Key Observation

The forest loss reached its peak in 2017 (2,012 ha), but has been on a declining trend since then. The 2025 figure of 1,742 ha represents a 13.4% improvement compared to the peak, indicating positive conservation efforts.

🎯 Summary & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Total Loss (2000-2025): 24,925 hectares (1.4% of original forest)
  • Peak Year: 2017 with 2,012 hectares of annual loss
  • Recent Improvement: 13.4% reduction in forest loss from peak to 2025
  • Highest Risk Zone: Badengaido (3,022 ha in 2025)
  • Conservation Status: UNESCO World Heritage Site with active management

Recommendations

  • Intensify conservation efforts in high-risk zones (Badengaido, Enjewe)
  • Expand core zone protection given lower loss rates
  • Implement sustainable practices in agricultural zones (ZAD)
  • Maintain monitoring systems and data collection beyond 2025
  • Engage local communities in forest protection initiatives
  • Scale up the positive trends observed post-2017
